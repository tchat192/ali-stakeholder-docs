Conversation Engine

Prompt V3.4.0 — Mark's Review

On March 4, Mark Mirra recorded a 16-minute screen recording testing V3.4.0 against two real scenarios. His verdict: "As a system to make decisions and give them a punchy output, I liked it." He identified 5 issues.

Issues Identified
Mark's 5 Issues from Video Review
▶
01
Double Questions High
Same question asked twice in a row. Occurred 4+ times. "This is now the 4th question... I don't know why we're getting the double question."
02
Too Many Questions Med-High
User fatigue after 5+ diagnostic questions before coaching output. "My gut says 3-5 max."
03
Timeline Hallucination Medium
Ali said "2-3 months left, present by mid-November" when test was in March. "That's odd."
04
Multi-Select UI Missing Medium
When asked for top three strategic priorities, Mark expected a multi-choice selector.
05
Responses Need More Depth Medium
"When Ali gives her responses at the end of a question sequence, they can be a little more robust and detailed."

Prompt Diff

V3.4.0 Current vs. Mark's Revisions

Mark submitted a revised prompt restructuring the coaching operating system. Key changes with recommendations below.

Section 1
Identity & Opening Frame
Adopt ▶
Before
You are Ali, a negotiation coach. [PROMPT_VERSION: 3.4.0] Ali coaches based on what you share. The better context you give, the sharper the guidance.
After
You are Ali — Aligned Negotiation's negotiation coach. [PROMPT_VERSION: 3.4.0] Ali's job is to help the user *negotiate better* through natural conversation. - You do not run surveys. - You do not interrogate. - You deliver value fast. - You stay ethical. - You are comprehensive and balanced. Ali's coaching is grounded in Aligned's Strategic Framework ASF: - Relationships (mindset + climate) - Goals (Goal / Authorization / Why zones) - Process (Prepare → Communicate → Propose → Align)
Adopt

Anchors Ali to the Aligned Strategic Framework and names the 4-phase process. The original identity was generic; revision ties coaching to methodology. "Comprehensive and balanced" + ethical guardrails strengthen brand.

Section 2
Coaching Operating System
Adopt ▶
Before
YOUR APPROACH: PROBLEM-BASED COACHING You coach negotiations through NATURAL CONVERSATION, not surveys. You identify problems, assess power dynamics, and offer strategic paths. CORE PHILOSOPHY 1. Identify the PROBLEM 2. Assess COLLABORATIVE or TRANSACTIONAL 3. Determine PROCESS STAGE 4. Offer STRATEGIC PATHS 5. Deliver VALUE FAST
After
ALI'S COACHING OPERATING SYSTEM (Problem-Based + Framework-Based) You coach by identifying the *primary constraint* first. Every negotiation problem is anchored in: 1) Relationships problem - Trust, rapport, tension, ego 2) Goals problem - Unclear priorities, weak walkaway 3) Process problem - Wrong phase, wrong stakeholders Your flow is: 1. Acknowledge 2. Diagnose: relationship/goals/process + phase + mindset/type 3. Teach using What / Why / How 4. Apply with language and tactics 5. Develop a strategic playbook 6. Refine as new info unfolds
Adopt

Mark's 6-step flow (Acknowledge → Diagnose → Teach → Apply → Develop → Refine) is more structured. "Develop" (playbook) and "Refine" (iterative) address "responses could be more robust." What/Why/How teaching is methodology standard ASF.

Section 3
Signal Extraction & Deal Mapping
Merge ▶
Before
SIGNAL EXTRACTION (Internal Only) The 10 Calibration Signals: 1. Goals Status 2. Relationship Quality (Spectrum) 3. Process Stage 4. Leverage Assessment (Spectrum) 5. Alternatives (BATNA) 6. Timeline 7. Complexity (Terms Count) 8. Tenure 9. Counterparty Behavior 10. Problem Type
After
WHAT YOU MUST EXTRACT (Silently) A) The Deal Map (minimum viable context) - Who are the parties? - What is being negotiated? - What terms are on the table? - What does success look like? - What alternatives exist? - What has happened so far? - What is the next interaction? B) Aligned Strategic Signals 1. Phase: Prepare/Communicate/Propose/Align 2. Mindset/type: Bargaining/Trading/Creating/Partnering 3. Goal discipline: Goal/Authorization/Why zones 4. Alternatives: BATNA and WATNA 5. Forces: Market/External/Internal 6. Climate: Non-existent to Trusting 7. Complexity 8. Timeline pressure C) Red flags - Buying comfort with money - Negotiating against themselves - Stuck on single term - Ego driving decisions
Merge

"Deal Map" and Red Flags are strong additions. But current 10-signal framework provides more granular detection (Tenure, Leverage spectrum). Keep 10 signals as internal calibration, add Deal Map as initial assessment model, add Red Flags as coaching guardrail. Climate spectrum (Non-existent → Trusting) is more nuanced than current binary.

Section 4
Value Delivery Requirements
Adopt ▶
Before
RESPONSE STRUCTURE First Response (After Opening): Deliver a tactical insight AND a targeted follow-up. Never respond with only a question. Third Response (Insight Required): By now, you MUST deliver value: "Here's what I'm seeing: [Problem]. Given [situation], options: [Paths]. Which direction feels right?"
After
VALUE DELIVERY TEMPLATES (Use to ensure Exchange 3 value) By Exchange 3, you must produce one of: A) The Quick Read + Next Move - What I think is happening (1-2 sent.) - The primary constraint - The next move (1-2 actions) - A script (exact language) B) The 4-Phase Mini Plan - Prepare: what to decide - Communicate: what to learn - Propose: what to offer (MESO) - Align: how to lock it in C) The "If-Then" Trade Builder - If you ___, then we ___. - What we need in return - Why it's reasonable
Adopt

Three concrete output formats (Quick Read, 4-Phase Mini Plan, If-Then Trade Builder) vs. vague "deliver value." Directly addresses Issue #5 ("more robust"). Templates reinforce methodology by naming phases and trades. ASF

Section 5
Scope Boundaries & Guardrails
Adopt ▶
Before
Ali is NOT a business consultant, financial advisor, or legal counsel. Ali does NOT Provide: - Equity/pricing recommendations - Financial advice - Contract redlining - Deep-dive business analysis - Legal recommendations - Valuation assessments
After
NON-NEGOTIABLES F. Scope boundaries. You are not: - Legal counsel, but can offer guidance on how to work with Legal. - Financial advisor, but can offer guidance on common pricing practices. - Contract redliner, but can offer guidance on how to approach redlines. - Pricing specialist, but can offer insights on choosing a pricing strategy.
Adopt

"But can offer guidance on..." pattern redirects users toward what Ali CAN help with. Softens boundary without breaking scope. Ethics guardrail ("push back and outline consequences") stronger than current "offer alternatives."

Section 6
Preferred Tactics
Merge ▶
Before
ALIGNED METHODOLOGY VOICE ~1 in 4 coaching turns: drop a casual reference (Munger, Sun Tzu, Machiavelli) Other 3 in 4: use concepts invisibly. NEVER say "Research shows..." Source authors are intellectual ancestry.
After
ALIGNED TACTICS YOU SHOULD PREFER Use these frequently, with scenario-specific language: - Clear, Assertive, Respectful presence - Calibrated questions (What/How) - Firm vs. flexible language - Anchoring (go first unless you can't) - Conditional trading ("If you, then we") - MESOs (multiple equivalent offers) - Summaries to build momentum - Time-outs and silence - Total value mindset
Merge

Explicit tactics list (MESOs, conditional trading, anchoring) gives Ali a concrete toolkit. But current voice guidelines (intellectual ancestry, no academic citations) define Ali's personality — preserve those. Add tactics list as "preferred toolkit" section. ASF

Overall Assessment

Recommendation: Merge with bias toward adoption. Mark's revision adds structured value delivery templates, explicit methodology grounding (ASF phases, zones, forces), and softer scope boundaries with redirect paths. Current prompt's strengths — voice guidelines, intellectual ancestry, path options framework, coaching examples — should be preserved and merged. Target: V3.5.0.

Phase 2 Module

Contract Decomposition

Tom: "I know very little about the beef commodity industry. There's terminology in the contract I had no context of." This module reads raw contracts and pre-populates Tom's 4-phase planning workbook.

Architecture
3-Stage Pipeline
▶
Stage 1
Anonymize
Local-only PII stripping, OCR for scanned PDFs (Tesseract 300dpi). No raw documents leave user's machine.
Built
Stage 2
Schema-Driven Extract
Parsing + LLM extraction using Tom's methodology as schema. Per-field confidence + citation.
Build
Stage 3
Coach
Format into 4-phase workbook. Pattern Mirror: show what Ali sees, what it doesn't. Score relevant Aligned Moves.
Build

Why 3 stages instead of 5: Tom's methodology doesn't separate extraction, classification, and analysis — he does them simultaneously. The schema does this in a single LLM pass.

Business Case
4-8 Hours → 30 Minutes
▶

Contract-to-strategy prep time reduction. Internal dogfooding first. Chelsea (new Aligned employee) is primary test user — if a domain novice can use the output to prepare for a client engagement, the module works.

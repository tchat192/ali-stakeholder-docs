V3.4 System Prompt Changes

What changed, why, and what stayed the same. Prepared for Mark Mirra.

V3.3.0 → V3.4.0
Executive Summary

What's New in V3.4

Six targeted changes. No architectural rewrites. Core philosophy untouched.

1

43 Named Aligned Moves (Bug Fix + 8 New)

Fixed a critical production bug where the 35 existing moves were never injected into Ali's context. The wrapInXml() function existed but was orphaned from the streaming pipeline -- moves were defined in code but the model never saw them.

Added 8 new NSFD moves from Tom's review:

  • Calibrated Question -- open-ended How/What questions that shift control
  • Accusation Audit -- preemptively name negatives to defuse them
  • "That's Right" Target -- aim for "that's right," not "yes" or "you're right"
  • Black Swan Hunt -- discover unknown unknowns that change leverage
  • Leverage Triangle -- positive, negative, and normative leverage taxonomy
  • Rule of Three -- verify genuine commitment three different ways
  • Gentle No -- four-step decline sequence preserving the relationship
  • Reality Bend -- loss aversion + fairness framing + deadline psychology

Plus 1 restored move: Caution-Threat-Exit (was in voice instructions but missing from the array).

2

Priority Framing

Ali now asks "What's the ONE term you must win?" within the first 1-2 exchanges. This was Mark's Feb 12 request -- users were getting broad guidance without ever clarifying their single most important outcome. The priority term feeds into all subsequent tactical recommendations.

3

Counterparty Perspective

Before recommending tactics, Ali now explicitly models what the other side is thinking. This creates a "perspective rotation" step: what are their constraints, incentives, and pressures? Tactics are then calibrated to the counterparty's likely response, not just the user's position.

4

Anti-Probability Guardrail

Ali no longer generates statements like "60% chance they'll accept" or "there's a 70% probability this works." Tom flagged this in January testing -- percentage predictions create false precision and potential liability. Ali now frames uncertainty qualitatively: "likely," "plausible," "risky."

5

Softened Instruction Markers

Replaced 5 aggressive CRITICAL: markers with WHY-clause explanations. Anthropic's Claude 4.x guidance found that uppercase CRITICAL and MUST markers cause over-triggering -- the model treats every instruction as maximum urgency, resulting in verbose 4-6 point responses when 2-3 would suffice.

6

3 Example Coaching Exchanges

Added three diverse examples demonstrating desired behavior: concise, insight-first, one thread at a time. Anthropic's research shows 3-5 well-chosen examples can improve performance up to 40%. These examples calibrate response length, register, and coaching structure without adding new rules.

Changed

CRITICAL Marker #1 -- Alternatives Rule

BEFORE (V3.3)
**CRITICAL RULE:** Facts override feelings - "I feel locked in but we have 3 RFPs" -> HIGH (trust the RFPs, not the feeling) - "Only supplier for this component" -> Check timeline before concluding BATNA
AFTER (V3.4)
**Why this matters:** Users often FEEL locked in while having real alternatives. Facts override feelings -- always. - "I feel locked in but we have 3 RFPs" -> HIGH (trust the RFPs, not the feeling) - "Only supplier for this component" -> Check timeline before concluding BATNA
Changed

CRITICAL Marker #2 -- Timeline Calculation

BEFORE (V3.3)
**CRITICAL CALCULATION:** - If switching_time < renewal_timeline -> Alternatives ARE viable (not BATNA) - If switching_time > renewal_timeline -> True BATNA constraint
AFTER (V3.4)
**Timeline math determines real leverage:** If switching can happen before renewal, alternatives are viable -- even if the user perceives lock-in. - If switching_time < renewal_timeline -> Alternatives ARE viable (not BATNA) - If switching_time > renewal_timeline -> True BATNA constraint
Changed

CRITICAL Marker #3 -- Complexity Rule

BEFORE (V3.3)
**CRITICAL RULE:** Trust term count, not user framing - "It's just paper" + multiple terms mentioned -> Trust the term count - "95% of my energy is on price" + 12 terms listed -> Still TRADING (complex contract)
AFTER (V3.4)
**Why we count terms, not framing:** Users minimize complexity when they're focused on price. The number of terms on the table reveals the real negotiation type. - "It's just paper" + multiple terms mentioned -> Trust the term count - "95% of my energy is on price" + 12 terms listed -> Still TRADING (complex contract)

AFTER (V3.4)

AFTER (V3.4)
**Frame as directional, not definitive.** You never have 100% context -- and the user knows that. Hedging shows calibration, not weakness:
New

Priority Framing (Calibration Signal #1)

ADDED (V3.4)
## CALIBRATION SIGNAL: PRIORITY TERMS In the first 1-2 exchanges, identify the user's #1 non-negotiable: "Before we go further -- what's the ONE term you absolutely must win? Everything else we can be creative with." Why this matters: Users who clarify their priority early negotiate with more focus. Without this, coaching spreads across too many terms and the user leaves without a clear action plan. Route all subsequent tactical recommendations through this priority. If their priority shifts mid-conversation, acknowledge the shift explicitly.
New

Counterparty Perspective Check

ADDED (V3.4)
## PERSPECTIVE CHECK Before recommending tactics, model the counterparty's position: 1. What are THEIR pressures and constraints? 2. What does success look like from THEIR side? 3. Where are they likely flexible vs. locked in? Surface this to the user: "Let me think about their side for a second..." Why this matters: Tactics calibrated to the counterparty's actual situation outperform generic advice. Users who understand the other side's constraints find creative solutions faster.
New

Anti-Probability Guardrail

ADDED (V3.4)
## PROBABILITY GUARDRAIL Do NOT generate percentage predictions about negotiation outcomes: WRONG: "There's a 60% chance they'll accept this offer." WRONG: "I'd put the probability at 70% that they budge on price." RIGHT: "Based on what you've described, they're likely to push back on price but may have flexibility on timeline." RIGHT: "This approach has a strong chance of working given their constraints." Why: Percentage predictions create false precision about inherently unpredictable human interactions. They also create potential liability. Frame uncertainty qualitatively, not quantitatively.
New

3 Example Coaching Exchanges

ADDED (V3.4)
## EXAMPLE EXCHANGES These calibrate desired coaching behavior: --- **Example 1: Concise insight, one thread** User: "They want us to drop price 15% on the renewal." Ali: "Before we talk about the number -- have you mapped what they'd lose by switching? Implementation costs, ramp-up time, relationship equity. That's your leverage. Let's put a dollar figure on their switching cost, then we'll know if 15% is a negotiation or an exit." --- **Example 2: Diagnostic question before tactics** User: "My landlord wants to raise rent 20% and I have 60 days." Ali: "Sixty days is tight but workable. Quick question -- could you actually move if you needed to? Because that answer changes everything about how we approach this." --- **Example 3: Expert mode, validate and add nuance** User: "Procurement is running a reverse auction across 4 vendors. I've been their supplier for 6 years. Thinking about anchoring with a slight increase to signal confidence." Ali: "Your read is right -- a slight increase signals you're not desperate. But before you anchor, have you talked to anyone outside procurement? Six years means you have champions in operations. If you can get their internal stakeholders to advocate before the auction starts, you're playing a different game entirely."

Stability Report

What Didn't Change

Your core architecture is intact. V3.4 is a refinement, not a rewrite.

Core Philosophy (5-Step Framework)

Signal Extraction, Inference Rules, Classification Decision Flow, Confidence Calculation, Conversational Flow -- all unchanged. The fundamental architecture that converts natural conversation into negotiation classification remains exactly as designed.

10 Calibration Signals

All 10 signals unchanged. Priority Terms added as a new first signal; the existing 9 shifted in numbering but retain identical logic. No signal was removed or redefined.

Inference Rules (6 Edge Cases + 3 Extensions)

Rules 1-9 unchanged. Facts Override Feelings, Complexity Overrides Attention, Tenure Threshold, Current Behavior Overrides History, Choice vs. Constraint, Temporary vs. Structural, Market Structure Dominates, Timeline Math, Co-Investment Signals -- all identical.

Path Options (High / Balanced / Low Power)

The three power-path routing system is untouched. Users still receive different tactical recommendations based on their relative power position.

Transparency Scale

The graduated transparency framework (what to share, what to withhold, when) remains the same.

Phase-Appropriate Coaching (5 Phases)

The 5-phase coaching model (Prepare, Open, Exchange, Close, Implement) continues to drive which tactics Ali surfaces at each stage.

Expert Mode Detection

When a user demonstrates 15+ years of experience and correct terminology, Ali still skips basics, validates their read, and adds nuanced value. Detection logic unchanged.

Tactical Consistency Guardrails

Confidentiality protection (never reveal user's floor/ceiling to counterparty role-play) and pricing consistency (same prices throughout a conversation) -- both unchanged.

Business Advice Guardrails

Scope boundaries (Ali coaches communication, not business strategy) and redirect patterns remain intact. The anti-probability guardrail was added to this section, not a replacement. Tom's Jan 20 malpractice risk documentation untouched.

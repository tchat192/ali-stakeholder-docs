Proposed UI Changes

Profile → Top Right

Removes redundancy from main navigation. Profile is a secondary action—users don't need it front and center. Moving to top-right follows platform conventions (Gmail, Slack, every SaaS app).

Research: NNGroup on Navigation Patterns →

Navigation → Left Side

"Vertical navigation is a good fit for broad or growing information architectures... should be left-aligned, keyword front-loaded, and visible." This is the pattern users know from ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini—we're not inventing new paradigms, we're meeting users where they are.

Research: NNGroup Left-Side Vertical Navigation →

"Chat with Ali" → "New Deal" or "New Negotiation"

Emphasizes negotiation focus over generic chat. The current label undersells Ali's purpose. Question: which framing resonates more with our ICP?

Deal Point Tracker (Right Panel)

Dedicated space for methodology reinforcement. As users discuss their negotiation, the right panel surfaces key signals, tracks deal points, and reminds them of Aligned methodology moments.

Vision: Three-Column Layout

The industry is converging on a pattern: Left (navigation/history), Center (conversation), Right (context/artifacts). Claude's CoWork, ChatGPT, Gemini all use variations.

For Ali, this becomes: Left (Deal list), Center (Chat with Ali), Right (Deal Point Tracker / Methodology reinforcement).

Marketing Positioning & Language

How we talk about Ali matters. The AI amplifies what makes Aligned unique—it doesn't define us.

Positioning Thesis

Ali changes how people prepare for and navigate high-stakes conversations. Not by replacing expertise, but by making it available when it matters most—adaptive, personal, and grounded in how negotiation actually works.

We don't sell features. We don't compete on price. We build confidence in moments that shape outcomes.

The methodology is the hero. AI is the enabler.

Section 2
Problem / Solution
Initial Draft

You've got a big negotiation coming up. Maybe it's a salary discussion, a vendor contract, or a partnership deal. You know it matters. You're just not sure if you're approaching it the right way.

Ali is your negotiation coach — available 24/7, built on years of Fortune 500 expertise.

Option A — "The Methodology Gap"

The methodology gap.

You invest in training. You attend the workshops. And then the negotiation happens — and the preparation fades. The frameworks you learned don't translate in the moment. The patterns you studied don't match what's unfolding in front of you.

Ali closes that gap. Two decades of refined negotiation methodology, made adaptive. It learns how you think, calibrates to your style, and coaches you through the nuances that generic preparation misses.

Strategic intent

"The methodology gap" names the core problem we solve — workshop training fades within days. It's a phrase only Aligned can credibly own because it requires having the methodology in the first place. "Made adaptive" signals AI capability without saying "AI-powered." The technology is invisible; the value is front and center.

Option B — "Preparation Is Not Enough"

Preparation is not enough.

You can study every framework, model every scenario, and walk in with a plan. But negotiations don't follow plans. They follow people — their patterns, their pressure points, their pivots.

Ali is a negotiation intelligence platform built on methodology that has shaped outcomes at the highest levels of business. It doesn't just prepare you. It sharpens how you read the room, adapt in real time, and close with clarity.

Strategic intent

This direction challenges the reader's current approach — and positions Ali as the next level beyond preparation. "Shaped outcomes at the highest levels of business" signals our heritage without listing company names. The word "platform" reframes Ali from "coaching app" to something more substantive and category-defining.

Section 3
How It Works
Initial Draft

Here's how it works:

  1. Tell Ali about your negotiation. Describe what you're negotiating in plain language. Ali asks a few smart questions to understand what kind of negotiation you're facing.
  2. Get the right strategy for your deal. Not all negotiations are the same. Ali figures out whether you need to push hard, trade smartly, create value, or build a partnership.
  3. Talk it through, map it out, or practice it.
    • Need to think out loud? Have a conversation.
    • Need a game plan? Map stakeholders, clarify your BATNA.
    • Need to practice? Role-play the conversation.
Option A — "Three Modes. One Methodology."

Three modes. One methodology.

1. Profile

Ali maps your negotiation style across seven dimensions. Not a quiz — a diagnostic. The same behavioral framework used in enterprise coaching engagements, now calibrated to you.

2. Strategize

Describe your negotiation. Ali classifies the type, identifies the dynamics, and builds a strategy grounded in methodology — not generic advice.

3. Rehearse

Practice the conversation. Ali plays the counterparty, adapts to your responses, and surfaces the blind spots you'd miss on your own.

Strategic intent

Single-word mode names (Profile, Strategize, Rehearse) make this feel like a real platform with distinct capabilities — not a general-purpose chatbot. "Not a quiz — a diagnostic" and "seven dimensions" signal depth and rigor. The unifying thread — "one methodology" — reinforces that every mode is powered by the same Aligned framework.

Option B — "How Ali Works With You"

How Ali works with you.

Understand your style.

Everyone negotiates differently. Ali's Silhouette assessment maps your natural patterns — how you handle conflict, create value, and build leverage. This becomes the foundation for everything that follows.

Build your approach.

Bring Ali your negotiation. It identifies the type, the stakeholders, and the dynamics. Then it builds a strategy that fits your style and the situation — not a template, but a methodology-driven plan.

Sharpen your edge.

Run the conversation before it happens. Ali simulates the counterparty, pressure-tests your positions, and coaches you through the moments where deals are won or lost.

Strategic intent

"Works with you" positions Ali as a partner, not a tool. The language here is warmer and more narrative — it walks the user through a journey rather than presenting product modes. "Not a template, but a methodology-driven plan" and "where deals are won or lost" add weight and stakes to what could otherwise feel transactional.

?
Product-forward or narrative? The initial draft is instructional ("here's how"). Option A presents Ali as a platform with distinct modes. Option B tells a story of a partnership. Which better reflects how you'd explain Ali to a prospect at dinner?

What Makes Ali Different
Initial Draft

Why Ali is different

why this isn't just chatgpt

  • This isn't generic advice. Ali is trained on Aligned's methodology — the same frameworks used to coach executives at Fortune 500 companies.
  • It learns your style. Everyone negotiates differently. Ali adapts its coaching to how you think and communicate.
  • It's there when you need it. Whether your negotiation is tomorrow or in ten minutes, Ali is ready.
Option A — "Methodology, Not Models"

Not another AI assistant.

Methodology, not models.

Most AI coaching tools are wrappers on a language model. Ali is built on a structured negotiation methodology refined over two decades in high-stakes environments. The AI is the delivery mechanism. The methodology is the value.

Personal, not personalized.

"Personalized" means your name in a template. Ali goes deeper. It understands your Silhouette — your natural negotiation patterns — and calibrates its coaching to how you actually think and operate under pressure.

Compounding, not static.

Every session, every scenario, every outcome you track makes Ali sharper for you. This isn't a tool you use. It's a system that learns.

Strategic intent

"Methodology, not models" draws a clear line between Ali and every other AI product. The "X, not Y" structure across all three points creates a rhythm that's easy to internalize — and that the team can use internally too. "Compounding, not static" positions ongoing engagement as part of the value, which supports the subscription model.

Option B — "Built on Something Real"

What makes Ali different.

Built on something real.

Ali isn't built on prompts. It's built on a negotiation methodology refined across thousands of high-stakes engagements — salary negotiations, vendor contracts, M&A discussions, partnership structures. The AI preserves the rigor. The methodology provides the substance.

Tuned to you.

Ali doesn't give everyone the same playbook. It reads your behavioral patterns, understands your natural style, and adapts its coaching to match how you think — not how some average user thinks.

Gets sharper with use.

Ali learns from your practice, your outcomes, and your feedback. The more you use it, the more precisely it coaches. This is intelligence that compounds.

Strategic intent

"Built on something real" signals authenticity in a market flooded with AI wrappers. This version names specific negotiation types (salary, vendor, M&A, partnerships) so readers see their own situation reflected. "Intelligence that compounds" could become a brand line — it captures the data flywheel in language a CEO would use in a pitch.

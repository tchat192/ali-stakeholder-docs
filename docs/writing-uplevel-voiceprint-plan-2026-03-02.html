Ole Lehmann's Voice DNA

The Template Structure

Voice DNA is a markdown file with four sections:

1. Writing Rules

Explicit directives about your writing style. Examples from Lehmann's own:

  • "Write in a punchy, direct style"
  • "Use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max)"
  • "Vary sentence length for rhythm"
  • "Use concrete examples, not abstract concepts"
  • "Lead with the insight, not the setup"

2. Formatting Rules

How the text should be visually structured:

  • Line breaks, paragraph structure
  • Heading patterns
  • List vs. paragraph preferences
  • Bold/italic usage patterns

3. Banned Phrases (5 Categories)

This Is the Key Differentiator

The banned phrases list is the most valuable section. It explicitly blocks AI-typical language:

CategoryExamplesWhy Banned
Filler openings"In today's world...", "It's no secret that..."AI crutch patterns. Signal lazy writing.
Hedge words"Arguably", "It could be said that", "Perhaps"Weaken assertions. Terrance's style is direct.
Hype language"Revolutionary", "Game-changing", "Cutting-edge"Empty marketing speak. Evidence over adjectives.
Transition cliches"Furthermore", "Moreover", "In conclusion"Academic stiffness. Use natural bridges.
Meta-commentary"Let me explain...", "As I mentioned...", "Think about it..."Unnecessary narration of the writing process.

4. Writing Samples

3-5 samples of your actual writing that the AI uses as calibration targets. The samples should represent your best work, not average work — the AI targets the quality ceiling, not the floor.

How It Works in Practice

# System prompt or project instructions: You are writing in Terrance's voice. Before generating any text, load and internalize the following voice DNA file: @voice-dna.md Rules: - Never use any phrase from the Banned Phrases list - Match the rhythm and structure of the Writing Samples - Follow all Writing Rules and Formatting Rules exactly - When in doubt, shorter is better
Tool 2

James Kemp's Voiceprint Plugin

Source: github.com/jamesckemp/claude-plugins • v1.6.7 • Full Claude Code skill

Kemp's voiceprint is not just a template — it's a complete stylometric analysis pipeline built as a Claude Code skill with 6 interactive phases.

The 6-Phase Workflow

1

Sample Collection

Gather 3-5 substantial writing samples (articles, essays, technical posts). The system analyzes them for voice fingerprint patterns.

2

Structural Analysis

Measures: sentence length distribution, paragraph structure, heading patterns, list vs. prose ratio, use of examples, opening/closing patterns.

3

Voice Fingerprint Extraction

Stylometric analysis of function words (the/and/but ratios), punctuation habits (em-dashes vs. colons), burstiness (long sentence followed by short), vocabulary tier (colloquial vs. technical vs. mixed).

4

AI Tell Detection

Cross-references your writing against a catalog of known AI patterns (ai-tells.md). Identifies which AI habits you naturally avoid and which might slip through.

5

Calibration Interview

Interactive Q&A using question-bank.md: "How do you start an argument?", "What's your relationship with qualifiers?", "How do you handle citations?", "What do you do when you're wrong?"

6

Profile Generation

Produces a writer-skill-template.md — a complete voice profile that can be loaded as a Claude Code skill or system prompt instruction.

The AI Tells Catalog

This is deeply useful. Kemp maintains a list of patterns that signal "AI wrote this" — patterns most writers never notice:

  • Function word smoothing — AI uses "the", "and", "but" at near-uniform rates. Humans are bursty.
  • Hedging clusters — "It could be argued that perhaps..." — AI stacks qualifiers.
  • List parallelism — AI makes every list item the same grammatical structure. Humans vary.
  • Transition uniformity — "Furthermore" → "Moreover" → "Additionally" — AI cycles through a small set.
  • Sentiment smoothing — AI avoids strong negative language even when critiquing.
  • Temperature artifacts — Unusual metaphors or word choices that feel "randomly creative."
Synthesis

Recommended Implementation for Terrance

Why This Matters for You Specifically

You write across at least 5 distinct registers:

  1. Prosecutorial analysis — The Disposition, policy criticism, structural arguments
  2. Technical architecture — PRDs, spec.md, stakeholder docs
  3. Investor/stakeholder communication — Memos, pitch materials, market analysis
  4. Research synthesis — Newsletter digests, academic papers, competitive intel
  5. Direct conversation — Slack, email, iMessage

Each register needs its own voice profile. A single voice-dna.md won't capture the difference between how you write a prosecutorial essay vs. a technical spec. But the foundational elements (banned phrases, sentence rhythm, argument structure) transfer across all five.

Implementation Plan

Step 1: Install Kemp's Plugin (15 min)

Clone jamesckemp/claude-plugins, copy the voiceprint skill to .claude/skills/voiceprint/. This gives you the full 6-phase analysis pipeline.

Step 2: Gather Writing Samples (20 min)

Collect 5 samples across your registers: 1 prosecutorial piece, 1 technical doc, 1 stakeholder memo, 1 research synthesis, 1 sharp email/Slack message. These should be pieces you're proud of — your quality ceiling.

Step 3: Run Voiceprint Analysis (20 min)

Execute the 6-phase workflow with your samples. The calibration interview (Phase 5) is the most valuable — it forces you to articulate preferences you've never named.

Step 4: Create Multi-Register Voice DNA (30 min)

Using the voiceprint output + Lehmann's template, create a voice-dna.md with sections per register. Shared banned phrases at the top, register-specific rules below.

Step 5: Integrate Into Existing Skills (15 min)

Add voice-dna.md reference to: prosecutorial/SKILL.md, ali-brand-artifacts/SKILL.md, newsletter-digest/SKILL.md, academic-paper/SKILL.md. Each skill loads the relevant register.

Step 6: Calibrate with Feedback Loop (ongoing)

When you read AI output and think "I wouldn't say it like that" — that's data. Use Kemp's refine workflow to correct the output, then update to feed corrections back into the profile.

Total setup time: ~90 minutes. Ongoing refinement happens naturally through use.

Draft

Preliminary Voice DNA: Terrance Chatman

This is a starting point based on observed patterns across your writing. It needs calibration via the voiceprint analysis.

Writing Rules

  • Lead with the conclusion or strongest claim. Context follows, not precedes.
  • Use evidence-first argumentation: cite the source, then interpret.
  • Vary sentence length aggressively. Long analytical sentences followed by short punches.
  • Prefer concrete over abstract. Name the company, cite the number, quote the person.
  • When critiquing, be precise about what's wrong. Don't soften with "perhaps" or "arguably."
  • Technical writing: assume the reader is smart but missing context. Give context, not explanations.
  • Use structural repetition for emphasis (anaphora), not adjective stacking.

Formatting Rules

  • Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences). Whitespace is not wasted space.
  • Use em-dashes for asides, not parentheses.
  • Bold for key terms on first use or emphasis points.
  • Tables for comparisons. Don't narrate what a table can show.
  • Code blocks for technical references, even in non-technical writing, when precision matters.

Banned Phrases

CategoryBannedUse Instead
Filler openings"In today's rapidly evolving...", "It's worth noting that...", "As we all know..."Start with the claim or evidence.
Hedge stacking"It could potentially be argued that perhaps...", "To some extent..."State it or don't.
Hype words"Revolutionary", "Game-changing", "Paradigm shift", "Unlock"Describe the actual impact with evidence.
Academic stuffing"Furthermore", "Moreover", "In conclusion", "It is important to note"Natural transitions or none.
AI patterns"Let's dive in", "Here's the thing", "At the end of the day"Just say the thing.
Sycophancy"Great question!", "Absolutely!", "That's a really important point"Address the substance directly.

Register Profiles

Prosecutorial

Strongest voice. Dense with citations. Builds cases with evidence chains. Uses structural repetition ("This is not... This is..."). Closes with actionable indictment, not summary.

Technical

Precise, sparse. Uses code examples and architecture diagrams. Assumes competence. States tradeoffs explicitly. No marketing language in technical docs.

Stakeholder

Warm but substantive. Leads with "what it means for you." Includes data but frames it narratively. Professional without being corporate.

Research Synthesis

Signal extraction, not summary. Highlights what's new, what contradicts prior understanding, what's actionable. Source attribution on every claim. Critical analysis, not neutral reporting.

Loading Pattern

Each skill that produces writing adds a single line to its SKILL.md:

## Voice Calibration @.claude/skills/voice-dna/base.md @.claude/skills/voice-dna/[register].md

This keeps voice DNA modular and reusable across skills without duplication.

The Feedback Loop

Over time, when you correct AI output:

  1. Use /voiceprint refine — paste the AI output + your corrected version
  2. The system identifies what changed and why
  3. Use /voiceprint update — feeds corrections into the voice profile
  4. Corrections accumulate, making the profile progressively more accurate

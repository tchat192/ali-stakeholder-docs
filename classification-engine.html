The Five Negotiation Types

Ali classifies every negotiation into one of five types, each with a different coaching approach:

BATNA
Deficiency

No alternatives.
Risk management mode.

Bargaining

Commoditized.
Conditional offers.

Trading

Multiple terms.
Strategic trade-offs.

Creating

Expanding value.
Joint opportunities.

Partnering

Strategic alliance.
Long-term investment.

Six Signals Ali Extracts

From a single conversational prompt, Ali infers these signals to classify:

1. ALTERNATIVES (BATNA)

Can they switch suppliers?

HIGH MEDIUM LOW

2. TIMELINE

How urgent is the decision?

RELAXED NORMAL URGENT

3. COMPLEXITY

How many terms being negotiated?

1-2: Simple 3-5: Medium 6+: Complex

4. TENURE

How long have they worked together?

<2yr: NEW 2-5yr: EMERGING 5yr+: ESTABLISHED

5. TRUST

What's the relationship quality?

HIGH MEDIUM LOW DEGRADED

6. JOINT VALUE

Are they building something together?

NONE EXPLORING IMPLEMENTED

Classification Decision Flow

After extracting signals, Ali follows this logic:

Step 1: BATNA Check
→
Alternatives LOW + Tight timeline → BATNA Deficiency
Step 2: Complexity Check
→
Terms ≤ 2 + Price dominant → Bargaining
Step 3: Relationship Check
→
Tenure < 2yr OR Trust LOW → Trading
Step 4: Depth Check
→
Joint Value EXPLORING → Creating Joint Value IMPLEMENTED + 5yr+ → Partnering

Eight Inference Rules (Edge Case Handling)

These rules prevent common misclassifications - the nuance that makes Ali smart:

1

Facts Override Feelings

If user "feels locked in" but has RFPs → trust the RFPs

"I feel trapped... but we have 3 other bids" → HIGH alternatives
2

Complexity Overrides Attention

If user focuses on price but contract has 12 terms → trust term count

"It's just about price" + SLAs, volume, payment terms → Trading
3

Tenure Threshold Is Hard

Even excellent relationships < 2 years → Trading max

Amazing vendor at 18 months → "Trajectory toward Creating" (not there yet)
4

Current Behavior Overrides History

7-year relationship getting aggressive → treat as Trading

"Great for years but now hardball" → Degraded trust → Trading
5

Choice ≠ Constraint

Single source by preference (not lock-in) → alternatives exist

"We only use them because they're great" → MEDIUM alternatives
6

Temporary ≠ Structural

Emergency constraint in commodity market → Bargaining, not BATNA

"Need it in 2 weeks, only they can deliver" + 10 suppliers long-term → Bargaining
7

Market Structure Dominates Relationship

20 suppliers + great relationship → still Bargaining

"Incredible service for 4 years" but true commodity → Bargaining
8

Timeline Math Overrides Perception

If switching time < renewal time → alternatives ARE viable

"Locked in" but 6mo switch time, 8mo renewal → Not BATNA

Edge Cases for Discussion

Case 1: The "Locked In" Feeling

"We need to renegotiate our SAP contract. I feel completely locked in. But we did get RFPs from Oracle, Microsoft, Workday. Migration would take 18 months, contract renews in 24 months."

BATNA Deficiency → Trading

Rule 1: Facts (RFPs exist, timeline works) override feelings

Case 2: The Dormant Partnership

"7-year relationship. Back in 2019-2021, we did joint business planning, shared KPIs. Last 2 years: purely transactional. Now they're getting aggressive on pricing."

Partnering → Trading

Rule 4: Current behavior (aggressive) overrides history

Case 3: The Helpful Commodity Supplier

"Standard fasteners - bolts, screws. 20 companies make these. But we've been with this vendor 4 years - incredibly helpful, optimizing our inventory, really consultative."

Creating → Bargaining

Rule 7: Market structure (20 suppliers = commodity) dominates relationship

Open Question: Almost-Partnership

"3 years together, excellent performance. Now discussing joint inventory optimization - they want to co-invest in a facility near our plant."
